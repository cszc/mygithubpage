Hello World

Hi, I'm Christine. I'm currently pursuing an MS at the University of Chicago Harris School's program in Computational Analysis and Public Policy.

I like working out puzzles with code and data. I'm driven by solving civic and social problems that have positive human impact. Issues that excite me are often at the intersection of policy and technology, such as privacy, civic tech, and the governance of emerging technologies.

Last summer, I served as a fellow in the Mayor's Office of Chicago, where I analyzed data and wrote policy recommendations on initiatives related to sustainability, absenteeism, criminal justice, and self-driving cars. I also spent a good deal of the summer teaching policy wonks to code and honing my foosball skills.

In previous lives, I was an international development project coordinator for Education Development Center, a climate change organizer for the Union of Concerned Scientists, a lettuce farming intern in Massachusetts, and an apprentice builder of solar hot air panels. In 2013, I spent the better part of the year in Xi'an China as a Fulbright-Hays Scholar.

When I'm not at my computer, I like to be far, far away from my computer. That usually means hiking, camping, or just being outdoors.

Selected Projects

Meet Halfway

www.meethalfway.io

Meet Halfway finds mutally convenient meeting destinations based on starting locations, travel methods, and preferred meeting location type. The platform preserves privacy so that you can meet friends, strangers, or acquaintances without revealing your address. Suggestions are provided with fairness scores and approximate travel times to help inform your decision. Meet Halfway currently works for addresses within the United States.

Django, PostgreSQL, Bootstrap, JavaScript, Google Maps API
Find the code on github.

Detecting Bias in Police Complaints with Machine Learning

This project sought to detect potential bias in police investigations using differentiated feature sets with supervised machine learning. The project tested the hypthesis that the inclusion of complainant demographic data would boost the predictive abilities of modeling the outcome of police misconduct complaints in Chicago. Read the report here.

Python, Pandas, SciKitLearn, PostgreSQL, PostGIS, Matplotlib, NetworkX, Geocoding with Google Maps API

South Side Civic Scope-a-thon, October 2016

As co-president of South Side Civic, a CAPP-student led group at UChicago, I helped organize the second annual Civic ScopeAthon. Part hackathon, part case competition, the Civic ScopeAthon's main objective is to help students learn problem scoping skills while competing to solve data and technology challenges for local civic organizations.
